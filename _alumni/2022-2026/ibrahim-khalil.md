Ibrahim Khalil is a graduating student of Computer Science at Ashoka University, specializing in social network analysis and computational social science. Over the past four years, he has worked on multiple projects involving machine learning, political data analysis, and Python-based research tools. His capstone project focused on mapping political networks in Indian news media using text mining and network analysis, providing insights into media polarization patterns. 

Ibrahim has interned at data-driven research organizations and contributed to open-source projects related to natural language processing. He is particularly interested in combining social science theory with computational methods to analyze societal trends. Outside academics, Ibrahim enjoys cycling, creating educational memes, and participating in tech workshops.
